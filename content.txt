The History Of graphic Tees

The bachelor undershirt
In 1904 the Cooper Underwear Company, later to be known as Jockey
showcased their "bachelor undershirt". This product was a long sleeved undershirt
advertised to be low maintenace with no need for saftey pins, needles, thread, and featuring no buttons

Rising popularity of the white tee shirt
The U.S Navy adopted the white cotton t-shirts as an undergarment for their uniforms
in 1913. The U.S. Army followed suit and so did other manual laborer ocupations.
It was until 1938 that the white tee shirt was advertised as outer wear. Sears called it the "Gob style"
Gob was slang for sailors. picking up even more popularity and acceptance in the 1950s, the white shirts
were seen on rebellious Hollywood characters of movies.
